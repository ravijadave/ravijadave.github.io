I believe every educator's
voice matters.

I'm a Registered Early Childhood Educator based in Toronto, currently working at St. Mark's Daycare where I support a diverse group of toddlers and their families every day. I came to ECE through a background in psychology — and that combination has shaped how I think about children, communities, and systems.

Over the past two years, I've worked across multiple Toronto childcare centres, building relationships with families from newcomer, racialized, and multilingual communities. I've learned that the work of early childhood education is inseparable from advocacy: the conditions educators work in, the policies that govern our sector, and the voices that get heard in those policy conversations all shape what's possible in any classroom.

That's why I'm drawn to work that bridges direct practice and systemic change. I want to use what I know from the ground — from documentation, family conversations, curriculum, and care — to inform policy, amplify educator voices, and build the kind of sector-wide community that makes change possible.

Teaching & Advocacy Philosophy

My practice is grounded in Ontario's pedagogical frameworks — How Does Learning Happen?, ELECT, and the AQI standards — and shaped by a deep commitment to equity, cultural responsiveness, and anti-oppressive approaches.

Philosophy without action is incomplete. I attend rallies. I write to ministers. I stay current on policy. These aren't extras — they're part of the job.

Community & Membership Engagement

Where relationships
become community.

Membership engagement is, at its core, about trust — making people feel seen, valued, and connected to something larger than themselves. That's also exactly what good early childhood education looks like. The skills are the same: listening well, communicating clearly, meeting people where they are, and building relationships that last.

Community Event
Squishy Bag Sensory Day
CNH–Winchester Day Care Centre · 2023

I co-designed and facilitated a community engagement event for families of children with sensory processing and fine motor challenges — coordinating logistics across families, staff, and the centre's leadership team.

I designed educational posters and a newsletter-style handout using Canva, translating developmental concepts into language parents could use at home. On the day, I facilitated the session, introduced activities, and created space for families to ask questions and connect with each other.

Several parents told me it was the first time they'd felt genuinely included in their child's learning process.

📎 Artifact: Canva poster + parent handout from event
Daily Outreach
Family Engagement Practice
St. Mark's Daycare & Wee Leprechauns · 2024–2025

A significant part of my daily work involves intentional family outreach — particularly with newcomer families, racialized caregivers, and multilingual households who often face barriers to participation in their child's care setting.

I build relationships through consistent, warm communication: sharing observations at pickup, translating program information into accessible formats, involving families in curriculum planning, and making sure every caregiver feels respected — not just tolerated.

This has resulted in stronger family participation, higher trust, and children who arrive feeling settled and supported.

Policy & Advocacy

From the classroom
to systemic change.

Early childhood education is a policy issue. The affordability crisis, the workforce shortage, the wage gap — none of it is accidental, and none of it gets better without advocates who understand the system from the inside. As a practising RECE, I experience these realities every day. That proximity gives me both the motivation and the credibility to contribute meaningfully to policy work.

Sector Knowledge

CWELCC System

Canada-Wide Early Learning & Child Care

I understand the CWELCC system's goals around affordability, access, and quality, and I've seen firsthand how fee reductions have impacted family participation. I also understand the gaps in implementation — particularly for underserved and racialized communities.

ECE Workforce Crisis

Staffing, wages & retention

Staff shortages, high turnover, and wage stagnation are not abstract statistics to me — they describe the daily reality of my workplace. I know why educators leave, and I believe retaining quality ECE professionals starts with policy that treats this as a skilled profession.

Ontario Pedagogy Frameworks

HDLH · ELECT · AQI Standards

I apply these frameworks in daily practice and use them to advocate for quality programming within my centres. These aren't abstract documents — they're the language of our sector, and I'm fluent in them.

Legislative Context

Bill 10 & sector accountability

I have a foundational understanding of Bill 10 and its role in shaping accountability and transparency in Ontario's child care sector, developed through both formal study and ongoing professional engagement.

Equity & Anti-Oppression

Not a module.
A way of working.

Equity work isn't something I completed — it's the lens I use every day. The families I work with are predominantly from racialized, newcomer, and multilingual communities. The children in my classrooms carry identities, languages, and lived experiences that the mainstream ECE system was not originally designed to centre. My job is to make sure that changes in my room, and to contribute to changing it more broadly.

I have spent my entire career in Toronto childcare centres serving primarily racialized, newcomer, and equity-deserving communities. This isn't incidental — it's central to who I am as a practitioner.

My Framework

Anti-Racist Practice

Completed formal anti-racism training. Applied through materials selection, classroom conversations, environmental design, and ongoing self-reflection.

Trauma-Informed Approaches

Completed trauma-informed practice training. Use this lens to understand children's behaviour, support self-regulation, and engage sensitively with families navigating significant stressors.

Cultural Responsiveness

Actively integrate diverse representation into curriculum, honour different family structures, and approach every caregiver conversation with humility, curiosity, and respect.
